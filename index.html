Welcome to the official website of Saba Ceramics. We are Silvia and Giulio. In our work, we explore the spontaneous effects created by fire and natural materials. We aim to create graceful, warm, and timeless designs for interior décor and everyday objects. At times, our practice blends pottery, painting, and sculpture.

We take a hands-on approach, avoiding techniques that distance us from the creative process. We often fire our pieces in a raku kiln or use wood firing with the saggar technique. Along this journey, we embrace ancient firing methods, making each piece truly one of a kind, with its own story to tell.

Nature is our main source of inspiration. Through our handcrafted pieces, we strive to reconnect people with the natural world. Each object is carefully crafted from the very beginning—from the design stage, to wheel-throwing or hand-building, to firing and, when needed, protective treatments to ensure long-lasting durability.

By choosing a Saba Ceramics piece for yourself or as a gift for your loved ones, you choose the quality of a unique object, born from our hands and minds from start to finish.

ABOUT US

Saba Ceramics is Giulio and Silvia, a couple shaped by a lifetime of shared experiences. Saba is the vessel where our paths meet: Silvia is a painter, a sculptor and an excellent cook; Giulio is a musician, researcher, and a ceramist.

The name 'Saba' comes from an ancient syrup made from boiled grape must during the harvest. Traditionally used for 'sabadoni', a dessert from our roots, it represents the heritage we cherish. We chose it to reflect the same rustic beauty and natural effects we seek in our work.

Giulio Galamini

"I am a ceramicist and a researcher. My work on the potter’s wheel is a search for elegance, inspired by the harmonious forms of antiquity and my passion for history. My background in Eastern martial arts naturally draws me toward Oriental principles, which I reinterpret through a Western European lens. As a geologist, I see ceramic firing as a natural process: a glaze melting in the kiln is much like a rock turning into magma. Techniques like raku and saggar allow me to experiment with these transformations, perfectly uniting all my passions in a single craft."

Silvia Farina

"I am a sculptor and visual artist. In my work, I explore forms of forest and other animal life, as well as trees and creatures with which I feel a deep connection. For Saba, I work as a designer and decorator, also handling firings and marketing."
